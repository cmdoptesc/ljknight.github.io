About Me

In 2011, I moved to San Francisco after two years of teaching English to kindergarten and elementary school kids in Seoul, Korea. Back in the US, I intended to change course and study coding and design in order to begin a career in web development. After considering my options, I discovered the only way to make this happen would require enrollment in a college degree program, take at least two years to complete, and offer dubious results. With an unused degree in advertising under my belt, I opted to pursue a career in marketing and social media instead.

This path proved to be a great experience. I became knowledgeable in the many facets of marketing for an e-commerce company, and was given the opportunity to build much of the social media presence from scratch. I loved the creative aspects of the job, including writing pun-filled Valentine's Day cards for our Facebook fans and making stop-motion videos to post on Vine.

Despite my passion for the job, I soon realized it wasn't challenging in the way I wanted it to be. After two years, I left my full-time position to travel around Europe and Asia for seven months while continuing to work part-time.

Upon returning to San Francisco at the beginning of this year, I joined a free front-end development class at Noisebridge, a hackerspace in the Mission. This class reignited my interest in coding, and I found myself reassessing my options. I was happy to discover that the opportunities for learning coding had grown dramatically since my first attempt, and soon enough I was researching programming bootcamps.

Once I decided to focus on Javascript as my first programming language, I knew Hack Reactor was where I wanted to be. After studying for a few months, I interviewed for the program, and the next day I was ecstatic to hear that I got into their August 2015 cohort.

Goals: Why I Want to Learn How to Code

  • I chose to learn Javascript first because it's ubiquitous and flexible. I will be able to use it for a wide range of projects and applications.
  • Although I was initially drawn to the front-end side of development, I am excited to learn more about back-end capabilities.
  • It can be very challenging, and I appreciate the way it forces me to think.
  • Building applications and solving problems allows me to be creative.
  • It's a field that's constantly evolving and being updated, so I will continue to learn throughout my career.
  • There's nothing like the feeling I get after I solve a tricky problem using beautifully concise code. And then I get to point at it and say, "I wrote that!"
